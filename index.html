Why Donate?

Organ donation is one of the most selfless acts a person can perform, offering the gift of life to those in critical need. When you register as an organ donor, you're creating a legacy that can transform tragedy into hope. Each year, thousands of lives are saved through transplantation, giving recipients a second chance at life and allowing them to return to their families, careers, and passions.

Facts About Organ Donation

One Donor Can Save Up to 8 Lives

A single organ donor can save up to eight lives through the donation of vital organs including the heart, two lungs, liver, pancreas, two kidneys, and intestines. Additionally, tissue donation can enhance the lives of over 75 people through corneas, skin, bone, heart valves, and connective tissues.

Anyone Can Be a Donor

People of all ages and medical histories should consider organ donation. Your medical condition at the time of death will determine what organs and tissues can be donated. Even those with serious health conditions can sometimes be donors. Medical professionals evaluate each potential donor on a case-by-case basis.

It's a Simple Process

Registering as an organ donor is quick and straightforward. You can sign up online in minutes through your state's donor registry, or during your next driver's license renewal. Once registered, be sure to inform your family of your decision to ensure your wishes are known and respected.

Ready to Make a Difference?

By registering as an organ donor today, you're making a commitment to potentially save multiple lives in the future. It takes just a few minutes to register, but the impact of your decision can last generations.
