What is the RCAF?

The Royal Canadian Air Force (RCAF) is the air and space force of Canada. Established on April 1, 1924, it protects Canadian sovereignty and contributes to peace worldwide. The RCAF performs vital missions including surveillance, air defence, search and rescue, and international operations.

RCAF Through the Years

  • 1924: RCAF officially established on April 1.
  • 1939-1945: RCAF becomes the fourth-largest Allied air force during WWII.
  • 1940: Launch of the British Commonwealth Air Training Plan.
  • 1950s: Participation in NATO and NORAD operations.
  • Present: Modernization with advanced aircraft and global missions.

Notable Figures

Air Marshal Billy Bishop

Canada's top flying ace in WWI with 72 victories. Instrumental in establishing the BCATP during WWII and continued to shape the RCAF.

Modern RCAF

The RCAF operates a diverse fleet of aircraft to fulfill national and international missions:

  • CF-18 Hornet – Multi-role fighter
  • CC-130 Hercules – Tactical airlift
  • CH-147F Chinook – Heavy-lift helicopter
  • CC-177 Globemaster III – Strategic airlift
  • CH-148 Cyclone – Maritime helicopter
