What is the RCAF?

The Royal Canadian Air Force (RCAF) is the air and space force of Canada. Established on April 1, 1924, it protects Canadian sovereignty and contributes to peace worldwide. The RCAF performs vital missions including surveillance, air defence, search and rescue, and international operations.

Timeline of the RCAF

  • 1924 – RCAF is Founded

    Canada formally establishes the Royal Canadian Air Force to manage its growing aviation needs.

  • 1939-1945 – WWII Involvement

    Over 250,000 Canadians serve; RCAF becomes the fourth-largest Allied air force, crucial in bombings and reconnaissance missions.

  • 1940s – BCATP Established

    Canada trains 130,000+ Allied aircrew in 107 schools, earning global praise and the nickname "Aerodrome of Democracy".

  • Present – Modern RCAF

    With advanced aircraft and global missions, the RCAF remains vital to Canada’s national defence and international cooperation.

Did You Know?

The RCAF flew over 1 million sorties in WWII.
The RCAF has over 390 active aircraft today.
Billy Bishop, Canada’s top flying ace, had 72 confirmed victories.
Canada trained more than 130,000 aircrew from Allied nations.
The RCAF was once the 4th largest air force in the world.
